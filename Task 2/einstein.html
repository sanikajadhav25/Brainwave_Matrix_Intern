Albert Einstein

Theoretical Physicist

Albert Einstein (1879-1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics (alongside quantum mechanics). His mass-energy equivalence formula, E=mc², is one of the most famous equations in history.

Major Contributions

Einstein made groundbreaking contributions to science, including:

  • The Special and General Theory of Relativity.
  • The Photoelectric Effect, which earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921.
  • Brownian Motion, providing evidence for the existence of atoms.
  • The concept of Space-Time as a single entity.

Legacy

Einstein's work reshaped the understanding of time, space, and gravity. His discoveries led to advancements in quantum mechanics, nuclear energy, and cosmology. Today, he remains one of the most influential scientists in history.
