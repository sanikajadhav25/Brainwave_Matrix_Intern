Nikola Tesla

Inventor, Electrical Engineer, and Futurist

Nikola Tesla (1856-1943) was a Serbian-American inventor best known for his contributions to the development of alternating current (AC) electricity. His ideas and inventions laid the foundation for modern electrical power systems and wireless communication.

Major Contributions

Tesla's groundbreaking work includes:

  • Development of the AC (Alternating Current) electrical system.
  • Invention of the Tesla Coil, which revolutionized wireless transmission.
  • Advancements in radio wave technology, influencing modern communication.
  • Early concepts of wireless power and renewable energy.

Legacy

Tesla's work was ahead of its time, and although he faced financial struggles, his innovations continue to shape modern technology. Today, he is celebrated as one of history's greatest inventors, inspiring advancements in energy, wireless communication, and electromagnetism.
