Thomas Edison

Inventor and Businessman

Thomas Edison (1847-1931) was an American inventor and businessman known for his significant contributions to the fields of electricity, sound recording, and motion pictures. He held over 1,000 patents and was a key figure in the development of modern technology.

Major Contributions

Edison's most notable inventions include:

  • The practical electric light bulb, which revolutionized indoor lighting.
  • The phonograph, the first device capable of recording and playing back sound.
  • The motion picture camera, which laid the foundation for the film industry.
  • Developing the first industrial research laboratory in Menlo Park.

Legacy

Edison's innovations transformed industries and daily life, leading to the widespread use of electrical power and modern entertainment. His work continues to inspire inventors and engineers worldwide.
